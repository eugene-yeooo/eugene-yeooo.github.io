Human Skills: Learning Plan

Published 3 April 2025


What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term goal is to flexibly contribute to all the different sectors/industries that excite me, and I intend to achieve this via the career path of being an excellent programmer/software developer.


A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning:

Strengths:
  • 💪 Will try hard: I am capable of trying hard, being persistent, not giving up and embracing the challenge when engaging at the right level. This trait will be very useful when faced with challenging problems in learning how to be a programmer and trying to get unblocked/unstuck.

  • 💪 Focus-focussed:I can recognise when my environment is suboptimal for focussing, and what factors are not conducive to being productive. I know what it's like to be in flow state, and I like being in it. These strengths help with the flipped classroom approach, where I need to be self-driven and actively participate in my learning journey.

  • 💪 Efficiency-minded:I like streamlining my processes and being as efficient as possible, which aligns with the ‘refactoring’ aspect of problem-solving and thinking like a programmer. Also probably a good mindset in writing the foundations/frameworks of my code.
Limitations:
  • 📝 Distractions:When I am not engaging/engaged at an optimal level, I may be easily distracted and end up procrastinating instead. This is not ideal for the learning process, staying on-task and getting things done in a timely manner.

  • 📝 Fixations:When faced with a frustrating problem, I may spend too much time fixating on trying to solve it, neglecting other issues or tasks. Other times, I can be a bit of a perfectionist and focus too much on tweaking the minor details that may or may not even make a difference. This is detrimental to getting things done in a timely manner.

What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Communication - both verbal and written.


A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme:

I will manage my time well and stick to timeboxing my tasks. I will exercise self-control to not rabbit-hole into problems. I will ask for help when I need it, and I get better at recognising when I need to ask. I will focus on developing healthy habits (e.g. shutting out distractions, going to bed at a reasonable time/getting up early, tapping into my focus flow state, being intentional in my learning process and approach to problem-solving, etc).

When things build up and start getting to me, I will ask my family/friends for help with day-to-day routines (non-study related). I will have a serious chat with my facilitator about how things are going, and discuss how things can be improved. I will learn when to take a step back, take a breath, and give myself time to switch off and enjoy non-study related treats.


A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way:

If I am unable to solve an issue on my own within an hour, I need to ask someone for help - either my cohort peers, facilitator, or my own friends who are experienced programmers.


A description of what you expect from the Facilitation Team:

To not simply provide the solution, but to nudge me in the direction of properly understanding the problem and coming to a solution myself. This includes being able to ultimately understand how and why the given solution works.


Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in:

  • 🍜 Have dinner with my mum once a week
  • 🧗‍♂️ Climb outside 1-2 days per week (likely in the weekends)
  • 🏋️ Train at the bouldering gym 3-4 times per week, 1-2 hours per sesh. Flexible in the mornings, lunchtime or evenings.
  • 🫂 Spend time with my friends
