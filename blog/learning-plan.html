Human Skills: Learning Plan

Published 3 April 2025 | Updated 22 April 2025


Bootcamp Update

My long term goal and/or career pathway:

To find a career/employment where I can be optimally engaged in creative problem-solving, preferably in industries that are meaningful to me and/or have positive impact on the world.


What I think my biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp will be:

My biggest strength is my drive to take on programming challenges, and the ability to harness the excitement it fills me with.

My biggest limitation in Bootcamp is likely a human skills one, specifically in my ability to communicate ideas, my thoughts and feelings with my peers and coaches.


What I think my biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be:

I addressed the biggest non-technical challenge for me in the limitations question above, but I will likely also find challenge in speaking in front of a group, managing my time well with the more intense Bootcamp workload, staying focussed when engaging in Human Skills exercises (particularly with writing and formulating thoughts in a timely way), and generally maintaining good wellbeing with adequate sleep and a healthy diet.


What non-technical skills - human skills - I would like to see developed in myself while at Dev Academy:

While in Bootcamp, I really want to develop my communication skills, particularly in giving/receiving feedback well and in communicating my thoughts/ideas/perspective effectively. I would also like to improve my emotional intelligence, which by extension means working on my self-awareness, reflective skills and growth mindset behaviours.


What my expectations from the Bootcamp team are:

I have an expectation of the Bootcamp Team to effectively guide me through this steep learning curve, to assist me in improving the human skills outlined above, to help me conquer my limitations, and to facilitate me becoming a successful, well-rounded software developer.


My expectations of myself in Bootcamp:

In Bootcamp, I expect myself to show up and give 110%. I expect to not let myself be consumed by frustration, and to seek help within a healthy timeframe for any given block. I expect to be incredibly challenged by Bootcamp, but I also expect to have a strong growth mindset and not-yet outlook, and to embrace failure as an avenue for learning.

When it comes to workload, I hold myself accountable for sticking to timeboxes. I expect myself to manage my time well, and to balance my health and happiness throughout this course well. When I recognise signs of struggle that go beyond the technical sense, I expect myself to seek help in a timely way, either from peers, facilitators, friends or family.


Foundations Plan

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term goal is to flexibly contribute to all the different sectors/industries that excite me, and I intend to achieve this via the career path of being an excellent programmer/software developer.


A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning:

Strengths:
  • 💪 Will try hard: I am capable of trying hard, being persistent, not giving up and embracing the challenge when engaging at the right level. This trait will be very useful when faced with challenging problems in learning how to be a programmer and trying to get unblocked/unstuck.

  • 💪 Focus-focussed: I can recognise when my environment is suboptimal for focussing, and what factors are not conducive to being productive. I know what it's like to be in flow state, and I like being in it. These strengths help with the flipped classroom approach, where I need to be self-driven and actively participate in my learning journey.

  • 💪 Efficiency-minded: I like streamlining my processes and being as efficient as possible, which aligns with the ‘refactoring’ aspect of problem-solving and thinking like a programmer. Also probably a good mindset in writing the foundations/frameworks of my code.
Limitations:
  • 📝 Distractions: When I am not engaging/engaged at an optimal level, I may be easily distracted and end up procrastinating instead. This is not ideal for the learning process, staying on-task and getting things done in a timely manner.

  • 📝 Fixations: When faced with a frustrating problem, I may spend too much time fixating on trying to solve it, neglecting other issues or tasks. Other times, I can be a bit of a perfectionist and focus too much on tweaking the minor details that may or may not even make a difference. This is detrimental to getting things done in a timely manner.

What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Communication - both verbal and written.


A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme:

I will manage my time well and stick to timeboxing my tasks. I will exercise self-control to not rabbit-hole into problems. I will ask for help when I need it, and I get better at recognising when I need to ask. I will focus on developing healthy habits (e.g. shutting out distractions, going to bed at a reasonable time/getting up early, tapping into my focus flow state, being intentional in my learning process and approach to problem-solving, etc).

When things build up and start getting to me, I will ask my family/friends for help with day-to-day routines (non-study related). I will have a serious chat with my facilitator about how things are going, and discuss how things can be improved. I will learn when to take a step back, take a breath, and give myself time to switch off and enjoy non-study related treats.


A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way:

If I am unable to solve an issue on my own within an hour, I need to ask someone for help - either my cohort peers, facilitator, or my own friends who are experienced programmers. I should not shut myself in a cave and continue struggling alone, putting myself at risk of falling behind.


A description of what you expect from the Facilitation Team:

To not simply provide the solution, but to nudge me in the direction of properly understanding the problem and coming to a solution myself. This includes being able to ultimately understand how and why the given solution works.
