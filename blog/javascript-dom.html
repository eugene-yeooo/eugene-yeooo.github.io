Tech Explained: JavaScript and the DOM

Published 11 April 2025


An analogy to describe JavaScript and its relationship to HTML and CSS:

If HTML is the timber, the drywall, the floorboards and the general structure of a house, and CSS is the paint job, the wallpaper, the vinyl, the decor and the general design of the building, then JavaScript can be the power tools that put everything together and define the way we interact with construction of HTML/structure and CSS/design of the webpage/house.

In fact, JavaScript can also be thought of the appliances and smart home devices that people interact with in the home!


Explain control flow and loops using an example process from everyday life:

In JavaScript, control flow is the order in which instructions are carried out for interacting with a webpage. Its script usually runs top to bottom, but with control flow statements, you can manipulate the order of instructions - loops are a common example of such statements.

Take the process of driving as an analogy from everyday life - when you first get in the driver's seat, your control flow

could involve indicating if you need to pull out of a parking spot onto the road, or turning on your headlights if it's dark out, or turning on the wipers if it's raining. As you're driving, you should be looping the process of checking your mirrors, adjusting the wheel to stay in your lane, tapping the pedals to stay within an appropriate speed, and paying attention to potential hazards such as other vehicles or pedestrians.
