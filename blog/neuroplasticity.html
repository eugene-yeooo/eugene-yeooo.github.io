Human Skills: Neuroplasticity

Published 14 April 2025


What is Neuroplasticity?

Neuroplasticity is the concept that our brains are changeable like muscles in that you can grow from experience and reshape with the right training. It is a fact that opposes the neuro-mythological idea that our brains are hard-wired at birth, that what you got is all you'll get, and that your intelligence and ability to learn is fixed, unchangeable and predetermined.

The way neuroplasticity works revolves around the nature of neural networks in the brain. Neural networks consist of brain cells firing and connecting with each other in relation to particular behaviours or memories. The more often a particular memory is accessed or a behaviour acted out, the more often those brain cells fire and the stronger those connections become. This plasticity also works the other way - the less something is practiced, the less its relevant neural network is active, and those connections in the brain will be pruned away. In this way, you can physically train your brain, foster positive behaviours, and strengthen your learning. Key phrases include “cells that fire together, wire together” and “either use it, or lose it”.

Simply understanding the principles of how neuroplasticity works is beneficial to people. Research has shown that people's beliefs about themselves, and how they view successes and failures, have massive impacts on their actual performances and outcomes. As such, the simple realisation that one is physically capable of training their brain to be better at learning, and that intelligence is malleable, can be highly motivating for people to make the most of their brains, empowering people to adapt their behaviours in ways that foster the growth of positive neural networks, and maximise the full potential of their learning capabilities.

Aside from simply understanding and knowing the way it works, there are two other ways you can explicitly take advantage of neuroplasticity. The first is to practice a given activity or access a memory as much as you can - this causes those connections in your brain to fire much more, therefore building stronger and more hard-wired neural networks. The other way is to relate whatever new information you are learning to something you already know, to put these new concepts into context. This engages pre-existing neural networks in your brain, and makes connecting to these new learnings more accessible and stronger.

There are many ways you can increase and improve your neuroplasticity, but the general gist and underlying principle is to engage in activities that challenge your brain and stimulate new neural connections, and to sustain your brain in a healthy state of being. Stimulating activities could include learning a new skill, exploring new places and environments, engaging social connections, practicing mindfulness and meditation, or physical activity in general. To maintain a healthy brain, make sure you’re getting enough rest and quality sleep, manage your stress levels well, and have a healthy balanced diet.

What is Growth Mindset?

IQ refers to one’s cognitive and intellectual skills - their ability to reason logically, analyse critically, problem-solve skillfully, visualise fluidly, understand complex ideas and utilise the full potential of their memory.

EQ refers to one’s aptitude for self-awareness and empathy - their ability to understand and manage their own emotions and feelings as well as of those around them, regulate their state of mind, socialise appropriately, empathize and relate with others, recognise and determine their levels of motivation, and effectively build meaningful, positive interpersonal relationships.


Why is EQ important?

EQ is important because it defines the way in which we navigate the world around us, the challenges we face, the decisions we make, and the complexities in life.

Improving one’s EQ ultimately strengthens and improves one’s relationships, performance (both professionally and in whatever personal arena), emotional resilience, decision-making ability, communication, and happiness in life.
