Human Skills: Te Whare Tapa Whā

Published 14 April 2025


Te Whare Tapa Whā is model of health and wellbeing implemented with Te Ao Māori perspectives. It breaks down what it means to be human into 4 main dimensions:

  • Taha Tinana | Physical Wellbeing
  • Taha Hinengaro | Mental Wellbeing
  • Taha Wairua | Spiritual Wellbeing
  • Taha Whānau | Family/Community Wellbeing

The model goes on to suggest that these 4 dimensions share an underlying foundation called ‘whenua’, referring to the land, our surroundings, the environment, and the natural world. At the core of all aspects of our wellbeing, there is a connection to, an interaction with, a cause and effect flowing both ways with the spaces we occupy.


My Wellbeing Plan:

Here is my wellbeing plan based on the foundations and principles of Te Whare Tapa Whā - it consists of a list of activities I am committing to that relates to each dimension, strengthening in me each area of wellbeing:

Wall Activities
Taha Tinana
  • Rock climb outside once a week
  • Climb at the bouldering gym 3-4x a week
  • Maintain quality sleep and rest
  • Eat well
Taha Hinengaro
  • Engage the principles of growth mindset
  • Maintain/put more effort into my relationships
  • Take good breaks from studying
  • Avoid brain rot/scrolling on IG too much
Taha Wairua
  • Restart my weekly gratitude journal
Taha Whānau
  • Have dinner with mum once a week
  • Have movie night with my best friends once a week
  • Have date night at least once a week
Whenua
  • Pay more attention and have more appreciation for the intricacies and beauty in the rocks I climb, the canyons I descend, the caves I explore
  • Listen to and appreciate the native birds around me more when I'm at the crag, especially the pīwakawaka
  • Embrace walking around barefoot more
