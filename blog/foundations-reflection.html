Human Skills: Foundations Reflection

Published 21 April 2025



Three new things I've learnt about myself and my ego due to the Human Skills learning:

  1. With neuroplasticity and growth mindset, I am capable of growing, evolving, and learning well.
  2. I recognise when I've exhibited fixed mindset behaviour in the past, and now I know how to counter these negative tendencies.
  3. It has given me more explicit insight into who I am as a person, what my strengths and limitations are, and what I value.

The role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming:

Having good self-awareness and an explicit understanding of your values is an essential foundation to learning effectively. It fosters and encourages behaviours that optimise the learning process and make the most of engaging new material, especially with complex concepts in programming.

Empathy is important with the nature of programming work, not just in working as a team with colleagues, but also in the way you treat yourself. There will be many instances where you will be tested by blocks, frustration, failures, and conflicting perspectives on a project. The ability to be empathetic correlates to your mental fortitude and resilience in facing these challenges.

What surprised me the most about the Human Skills learning:

What surprised me the most was the amount of focus on shaping behaviours and mindset, and learning the weight Human Skills carries in building a successful programming career. It is interesting to hear that tech employers prefer a good human skills/culture fit over tech skills.


What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning:

There is definitely a mental hurdle in committing to some of the principles that shape our habits and mindset. There is a learning curve to understanding what it means to be human and how to make the most of our experiences, and it's important to acknowledge that it's okay to struggle.
